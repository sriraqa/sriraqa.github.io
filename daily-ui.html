Daily UI Selections

A collection of case studies created for the Daily UI design challenge.


Role: UI/UX Designer


Date: Jun 2022 - Aug 2022


Skills: UI/UX Design


View Projects





Introduction

The Daily UI was created by professional designers to inspire and challenge other designers from around the world. This challenge was started in 2016, and many participants post their designs on platforms like Dribbble or Twitter.

I decided to take on this personal project to learn more about designing UI and better my knowledge about user experience considerations.

These designs were created with potential consumers of some product as the main audience. Thus, the aim of these designs is to create a positive user experience that will lead the user to buy the product and come back again.

Below, I will show some selections of my work from the challenge and explain the cognitive reasoning behind the designs.

1. Credit Card Checkout Design

Created using Figma.


Ideation

Given the topic of "Credit Card Checkout" from Daily UI, I had three general ideas for what would be maintained in my design:


  1. Security - Since the user is giving personal information (such as address and credit card information), the company should ensure that the transaction is secure, and that the user is aware of this.


  2. Straightforward - When the user has reached the point of checkout, the process should be straightforward. The user should be able to easily go through with their payment without hinderances.


  3. Trustworthy - The company should convey trustworthiness to the users that will increase future loyalty.



Wireframe

The main features include shipping information, payment amount (including shipping), and payment method. I added a progress bar that shows the user how close they are to the end and makes the user want to complete the task. Various payment methods allow the user to use their preferred options to pay.


The Final Design

For the final design, I decided to use blue to make the user feel as if the payment is more trustworthy and secure. Additionally, the size, location, and shadows make the buttons more clickable, moving the user to continue.



2. Product Landing Page

Created using Figma and Photoshop.


Ideation

Given the topic of "Landing Page" from Daily UI, I had three general ideas for what would be maintained in my design:


  1. Vibrant - As a landing page, I wanted the design to capture the attention of the user. Moreover, having a unique page may have the user view the product as unique as well.


  2. Discoverable - The landing page should allow the user to easily discover products and purchase them.


  3. Informative - The landing page should showcase the products' features, so that the user can easily compare given features with other products.



Wireframe

I first drew low fidelity wireframes of the main screen with the positions of the featured product and text. I had two ideas of ways to showcase a product in a unique way. I decided on the second since it also leads the user to scroll down.



The Final Design

For the final design, I decided to use a varied colour palette. The background is muted, while there are cascading stripes to showcase the product and lead the user's eyes downwards. The "Buy Now" button is also highlighted with similar colours to show it will lead to the product.


The key features are noted on the headphones, and the name and description can be seen on the right. The options at the top are noticeable and clickable for the user.


Outcomes and Lessons

Overall, this challenge has allowed me to take on a wide variety of tasks that have helped me to learn about the typical conventions and considerations for each. I also learned how to design these interfaces with limited time, giving myself until the end of the day to complete the design.


As I complete more research from reading articles and books, I hope to include more cognitive considerations in my designs. Moreover, I would like to include more user research and personas to understand ways of how to improve accessibility and inclusivity in my designs.
